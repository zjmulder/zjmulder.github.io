Hey, I'm Zach

I'm an engineer, coder, data scientist, and outdoorsman who loves to tackle impossible problems. Throughout my life I've plunged headlong into parts unknown as I've explored analytics, modeling, web development, simulation, teaching, cancer treatment, cycling, and photography.

I graduated from UC Berkeley with a Master's Degree in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research in May of 2015 and have since been leading the development of a web-based application at Deloitte focused on regulatory calculations and reporting for clients that our tool exclusively provides in the market.

In my free time, I'm either out exploring on foot or on a bike with my camera strapped to my pack, or I'm engrossed in my latest side project to learn something new and utilize the data ever present in the world around me.

CSGO Scraper & Rankings

CSGO Live Predictions

Speaking of my Counter Strke: Global Offensive addiction, during my year in Graduate school I created a parser in Java that would read server logs from professional matches and convert the millions of lines of text into meaningful inputs to a regression based model focused on predicting round by round outcomes. Perfect information of the game allowed me to incorporate precise inputs like accuracy, team economy, or utilization of support items over time into the R based model.

Here's a writeup of the project.

Mobility Simulation

For my Capstone Master's Project, I worked with the WHO alongside Professor Lee Schruben to design and implement a simulation for a variety of mobility programs used to assign employees to locations throughout the world. I built the simulation in Excel using VBA to present a clean, intuitive interface for users to design and run their own experiments. Additionally, we created a number of metrics used to describe the behavior of the system at a high level and allow comparison of the different matching algorithms.

Here's the final report.

Dashboard for SONOS

During my last semester in undergrad, I led a group of five students in creating a high-level dashboard for SONOS, a wireless audio company, that depicted the overall health of the company through easily understandable visuals. I also personally researched new methods of forecasting demand using a modified Bass Diffusion model to increase forecast accuracy above 90%.
